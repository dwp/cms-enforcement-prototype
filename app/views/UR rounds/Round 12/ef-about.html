About

New view parent finances service available April 2025

View parent finances (VPF) service makes it easier for you to understand paying parents' finances and see the complete picture from the start of the case to the current date.

VPF gets data from the CMS2012 system and presents it in a simpler, clearer way to give you the information you need.

You can have both VPF and CMS2012 open at the same time and move between them as needed.

What can you do in VPF

You can view:

  • a summary of the paying parent’s finances so you can see how much a parent owes, what they've paid and when they didn't pay
  • a statement showing all transactions made in the case
  • payment plans to see what the parent has to pay, when and if we have received a payment
  • how much of the payment goes to child maintenance, collection fees and repaying debt
  • basic details about the receiving parent and children involved

How you get into VPF

You can only access VPF from within CMS2012. The button is available on the Contact summary screen only for:

  • case workers with permission to use the service
  • paying parent cases
  • paying parents who pay one receiving parent

How VPF differs from CMS2012

VPF uses simple language and avoids internal jargon. Labels on the page are self-explanatory and use the language case workers would use to explain the details to parents.

Finances page

For cases that have previously been on Direct Pay, CMS2012 includes collection charges that have beeen automatically satisfied for those periods in the total collection charges. VPF does not include auto-satisfied collection charges. The values you see will be exactly what the parent has paid or was expected to pay.

n CMS2012 you had to go to many screens to find information about charges and debt. In VPF we've provided a clear breakdown of charges so you can see at a glance exactly what charges or debt applied to the case. The possible charges are:

  • collection charges
  • enforcement charges
  • enforcement costs
  • CSA debt
  • DNA costs
  • subject access request costs
  • interest
  • fees
  • judicial review costs
  • appeal costs
  • CMS administration costs
  • Liability Order administration costs

This charges and debt detail appears only if the parent is expected to pay these specific charges.

Payment plans page

You can see the current payment plan up to the next annual review date. You can also view planned payments from the previous 12 months.

Cases page

If there are no children's names visible this may be because there are no active children on the case.
